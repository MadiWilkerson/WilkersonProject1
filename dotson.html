what is a dachshund?

The Dachshund is a small, long-bodied dog breed originally developed in Germany to hunt badgers and other burrowing animals. Known for their short legs and elongated bodies, Dachshunds come in smooth, longhaired, and wirehaired coat types. They are curious, brave, and loyal dogs with playful personalities, though they can be a bit stubborn. Despite their small size, they are energetic and require regular exercise and attention to stay healthy and engaged.
