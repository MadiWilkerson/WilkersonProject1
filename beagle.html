what is a beagle?

The Beagle is a small to medium-sized breed of dog known for its strong sense of smell, friendly personality, and distinctive howl. Originally bred in England for hunting rabbits and other small game, Beagles are scent hounds, meaning they track using their noses rather than sight. They typically weigh between 20-30 pounds and have short, smooth coats that often come in tricolor (black, white, and brown) or red and white patterns. Beagles are energetic, curious, and great with families, especially children, but they require regular exercise and mental stimulation to prevent boredom. Because of their powerful noses and determination, they can be prone to wandering if they catch an interesting scent. Overall, the Beagle is an affectionate, social breed that thrives on companionship and activity.
